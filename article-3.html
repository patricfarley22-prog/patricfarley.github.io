Why I Switched to Local AI (And Saved $200/Month)

Last year I was paying $200/month for AI tools. ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro, Midjourney, various APIs. The bills stacked up. And every month, I worried about my data going to some server I don't control.

Then I discovered local AI. Now I pay $0. And the results are better.

The Breaking Point

It wasn't one thing. It was death by a thousand subscriptions:

  • ChatGPT Plus: $20/mo
  • Claude Pro: $20/mo
  • Midjourney: $10/mo
  • OpenAI API: $30-50/mo (varies)
  • Various SaaS tools: $50-100/mo

Total: ~$200/month. That's $2,400/year. For a guy making $45K with $45K in debt, that's real money.

The Discovery: Ollama

I found Ollama — a tool that downloads and runs AI models locally. At first I was skeptical. "Surely cloud AI is better," I thought.

Then I tried Kimi K2.6 via Ollama cloud. It was faster than ChatGPT. More accurate than Claude. And it knew my local files because it was running on my machine.

My Current Stack

Here's what I run locally:

What About Quality?

I ran a test: I gave the same complex prompt to ChatGPT-4, Claude 3.5, and Kimi K2.6 (local). The results:

  • ChatGPT-4: Good, but generic. Felt like it was trying to be safe.
  • Claude 3.5: Better reasoning, but slower. Sometimes refused valid requests.
  • Kimi K2.6: Best coding output. Fastest. No refusals on technical content. Knew my project context because it has access to my files.

For my use case (building tools, analyzing crypto, automating tasks), Kimi wins. And it costs me nothing.

The Hidden Benefit: Privacy

When you use ChatGPT, your conversations train their models. Your trading strategies, your business ideas, your personal notes — they all go to OpenAI's servers.

With local AI, nothing leaves your machine. I can analyze my portfolio, brainstorm business ideas, and write personal content without worrying about data leaks.
